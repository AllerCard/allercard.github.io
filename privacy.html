Privacy Policy — AllerCard

Last updated: May 19, 2026

What we collect

Nothing.

AllerCard does not collect any personal data, usage analytics, crash reports, identifiers, or any other information about you. There is no account, no login, no email signup, no telemetry SDK, and no server we send anything to.

What stays on your device

  • Your allergen profile (name, allergens, severity levels, optional emergency contacts and medications)
  • The destination country you select
  • A timestamped record that you read and accepted the in-app disclaimer (kept on-device as a local audit log)

This data lives in your device's local storage only. AllerCard does not use iCloud sync, does not communicate with any server, and works fully in airplane mode. The translation packs that ship with the app are bundled at install time; nothing is fetched at runtime.

Permissions

AllerCard does not request any device permissions in version 1.0. No camera, no microphone, no contacts, no photos, no location, no notifications.

Third parties

None.

AllerCard does not include third-party SDKs, advertising networks, analytics providers, crash reporting services, or any external library that communicates with a server.

Translation sources

Allergen names are sourced from the European Union Regulation 1169/2011 (Annex II), the legal text on every food label in the EU. Sentence-level phrases are currently formal-register drafts written by the developer and are clearly marked as draft in the app's Translation Sources screen. The app discloses this honestly so users can decide how much trust to place in each language at any given time.

Children

AllerCard is suitable for general audiences, including parents managing allergen profiles for children. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children, because we do not collect data at all.

Your rights

Because no personal data leaves your device, there is no data for us to provide, correct, or delete on request. To remove all data tied to AllerCard from a device, delete the app.

Not medical advice

AllerCard is a translation aid for communicating food allergies abroad. It is not a medical device, not a substitute for medical advice, and not a guarantee of safety. Always carry any emergency medication prescribed by your physician (such as an EpiPen) and confirm ingredients verbally with restaurant staff.
